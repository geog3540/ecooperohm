Eleanor Cooper-Ohm Environmental Policy & Economic Analyst

My work focuses on understanding how natural disasters, governance, and social systems interact to shape patterns of migration and political violence. I analyze disaster impacts, mitigation capacity, and population movement using spatial data, demographic records, and geovisualization tools to uncover how communities respond to environmental shocks. These systems are deeply interconnected: disaster severity, state capacity, infrastructure, and local political conditions all influence whether people can remain in place, are forced to move, or become vulnerable to violence. By examining these geographic, temporal, and social components together, my research highlights how uneven mitigation resources, centralized governance, and environmental risk contribute to displacement and instability. Ultimately, I aim to produce data‑driven insights that support more equitable disaster policy and improve protection for at‑risk populations.
